Daśāvatāram — The Ten Avatars of Vishnu

TL;DR: The ten different forms of Lord Vishnu, each appearing at different times to protect the world and restore balance.

Source: Hindu mythology (Puranas, epics like Mahabharata & Ramayana, temple traditions)

Who they are

Daśāvatāram represents the ten major incarnations of Lord Vishnu. Whenever the world is in danger, Vishnu takes a new form to defeat evil and guide humanity. Together, these avatars show the journey of life — from simple beginnings to wisdom and spiritual liberation.

The Ten Avatars and Their Roles

  1. Matsya (Fish) – Saved the world from a great flood
  2. Kūrma (Tortoise) – Supported the mountain during the cosmic ocean churning
  3. Varāha (Boar) – Rescued Earth from the demon Hiranyaksha
  4. Narasiṁha (Man-Lion) – Destroyed the tyrant Hiranyakashipu to protect a devotee child
  5. Vāmana (Dwarf) – Humbled the arrogant king Mahabali with three giant steps
  6. Paraśurāma (Warrior with axe) – Destroyed corrupt kings to restore dharma
  7. Rāma – Hero of the Ramayana; symbol of virtue and righteousness
  8. Krishna – Divine teacher in the Mahabharata; guide, protector, and playful god
  9. Buddha – Preached peace, compassion, and non-violence
  10. Kalki (Future avatar) – Yet to appear, riding a white horse to end evil and renew the world

The Story

Whenever the balance of good and evil is disturbed, Lord Vishnu takes an avatar to protect dharma (righteousness). From saving the earth as a boar to teaching the Bhagavad Gita as Krishna, each form has a purpose. The Daśāvatāram is often seen as a cycle — showing how divine power evolves with humanity’s needs, ensuring that good always prevails in the end.

Modern Connection

The idea of Daśāvatāram is often seen as a symbolic “evolution” story — from fish (Matsya) to human (Krishna) to the future (Kalki). In popular culture, Kamal Haasan’s film Dasavathaaram (2008) creatively played on this theme, with the actor portraying ten different roles.
