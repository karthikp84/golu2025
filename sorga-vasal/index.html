Swargavasal — The Gateway to Heaven

TL;DR: The sacred doorway opened during Vaikunta Ekadasi, believed to be the path to Vishnu’s eternal abode.

Source: Hindu tradition, especially Vaishnavite temples in Tamil Nadu

What it means

Swargavasal literally means “Door to Heaven.” It refers to a special doorway in Vishnu temples that is opened only once a year, during the festival of Vaikunta Ekadasi. Devotees believe that passing through this doorway symbolizes entry into Vaikunta — the eternal home of Lord Vishnu — and grants liberation from the cycle of birth and death.

The Story

According to legend, on Vaikunta Ekadasi, Lord Vishnu opened the gates of Vaikunta to his devotees, allowing them to enter and be blessed. Temples recreate this event through the Swargavasal, a grand gateway that devotees pass through after long queues, prayers, and chanting. Walking through is seen as a symbolic journey to the divine, filled with faith and renewal.

Modern Connection

In major temples like Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, Srirangam and Parthasarathy Temple, Triplicane, lakhs of devotees gather each year for this event. The massive crowds, rituals, and chants make it one of the most spectacular celebrations of Vishnu worship in Tamil Nadu. Even today, people describe the experience of walking through the Swargavasal as deeply moving and life-changing.
