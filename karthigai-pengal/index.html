Kārthigai Peṅgal — The Six Divine Sisters

TL;DR: Six star-born sisters who raised Lord Muruga (Kartikeya) and are worshipped as symbols of nurturing love and divine protection.

Source: Hindu mythology (Skanda Purana, Tamil traditions, folk worship)

Who they are

Kārthigai Peṅgal (also called the Krittikās) are six celestial sisters linked to the six stars of the Pleiades constellation. They are honored in Tamil tradition as foster mothers of Lord Muruga (Kartikeya), the god of war and wisdom. Their devotion shows how motherhood and care extend beyond birth — to anyone who protects, nurtures, and loves.

The Six Sisters and Their Symbolism

  • Śivaṇḍī – Strength
  • Niththilai – Guidance
  • Ambarinī – Nurture
  • Māyāḍi – Compassion
  • Varuṇi – Protection
  • Chuppirakāi – Devotion

Note: Names and associations may vary in local traditions.

The Story

When Lord Muruga was born from sparks of Shiva’s third eye, the flames turned into six infants. The Kārthigai sisters found them and lovingly raised each child. Later, Goddess Parvati embraced all six together, merging them into one divine child with six faces — Arumugan (the six-faced Muruga). Since then, the Kārthigai Peṅgal are remembered as symbols of unconditional love, unity, and the nurturing power of women, forever connected with Muruga’s glory in Tamil tradition.

Modern Connection

The Kārthigai stars (Pleiades constellation) are visible in the night sky worldwide, making it easy to connect the myth to astronomy. In Tamil Nadu, they are celebrated during Kārthigai Deepam, where lamps are lit to honor Muruga and his celestial foster mothers.
