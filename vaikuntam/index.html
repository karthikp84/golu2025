Vaikundam — The Eternal Abode of Vishnu

TL;DR: The heavenly world where Lord Vishnu resides, filled with peace, beauty, and eternal bliss.

Source: Hindu mythology (Puranas, Vaishnavite traditions)

What it means

Vaikundam (also called Vaikuntha) is believed to be the divine abode of Lord Vishnu, the preserver of the universe. It is described as a place beyond sorrow and suffering, where devotees live in eternal joy with the Lord.

The Story

In the Puranas, Vaikundam is portrayed as a golden, radiant world where Lord Vishnu rests on the serpent Adisesha, with Goddess Lakshmi by his side. It is said to be free from time, death, and rebirth — a place of everlasting peace. For devotees, reaching Vaikundam means ultimate liberation (moksha), where the soul is forever united with God.

Modern Connection

The vision of Vaikundam inspires temple architecture, art, and rituals even today. During Vaikunta Ekadasi, temples open the Swargavasal (gateway to heaven), symbolizing the passage to Vaikundam. In songs, cinema, and discourses, Vaikundam continues to represent the dream of a world without suffering — a place of eternal love and divine grace.
